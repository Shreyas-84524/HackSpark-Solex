Legal & Privacy

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: February 21, 2026

Entity: SoleX Technologies (Mock Hackathon Entity)

1. Introduction and Scope

Welcome to SoleX. We provide B2B SaaS solutions for corporate shadow carbon accounting, ESG tracking, and green vendor matching. This Privacy Policy governs our collection, processing, and protection of digital personal and corporate data in strict compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 of India.

2. Our Role: Data Fiduciary vs. Data Processor

As a Data Processor:

When enterprise clients upload operational data (e.g., energy consumption, fleet logs) into SoleX to generate carbon cost sheets, the client remains the Data Fiduciary. SoleX acts purely as a Data Processor under documented instructions.

As a Data Fiduciary:

When individual users (e.g., CFOs, facility managers) create accounts, SoleX acts as the Data Fiduciary regarding their login credentials, business contact information, and platform usage analytics.

3. Information We Collect

  • Corporate Operational Data: Utility bills, supply chain manifests, and resource consumption metrics required to calculate Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions.
  • Financial Data: Aggregated operational costs to calculate the Green ROI and Shadow Carbon Cost.
  • User Account Data: Names, official email addresses, job titles, and audit logs of user activity.

4. Purpose of Processing (Lawful Basis)

Data is processed strictly for the following purposes:

  • Generating automated Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reports (BRSR).
  • Calculating financial risk metrics related to carbon tax and banking stability.
  • Matching enterprise requirements with verified green vendors (only executed upon the explicit affirmative action of the client).

5. Data Sharing & Third-Party Vendors

EcoLens does not sell corporate data. Data is only shared with third-party sustainable vendors (e.g., solar installers, eco-packaging suppliers) when a client actively requests a vendor introduction through our marketplace.

6. Data Principal Rights & Grievance Redressal

Under the DPDP Act, individual users have the right to access, correct, or erase their personal data, and the right to nominate a representative. For data grievances or withdrawal of consent, users may contact our designated Data Protection Officer (DPO) at:
